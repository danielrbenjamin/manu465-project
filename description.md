Course Project: Machine Learning Application to a Real-World Problem
Project Overview
As part of this course, you are required to complete a team-based course project that applies artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques to a real-world problem. This project is intended to simulate an industry or research-oriented ML workflow, from problem definition to model evaluation and reporting.

Students must form groups of 3–4 members and complete the project together.

 

Project Objective
The objective is to select a real-world dataset that addresses an industrial or engineering problem and to develop a machine learning solution for it. The focus is to practice skills such as:

Formulating a meaningful machine learning problem
Selecting appropriate datasets and algorithms
Applying machine learning techniques learned in this course
Analyzing results critically and communicating findings clearly.
 

Project Requirements
1. Dataset and Project Proposal (Initial Submission)
Choose a dataset related to a real-world problem, preferably connected to manufacturing, aerospace, automotive, healthcare, energy, or another industrial/engineering domain. The dataset should have sufficient size and complexity to allow meaningful machine learning analysis. You may use publicly available datasets or data provided by an industry partner (if applicable). After dataset selection, write a project proposal to explain your objective. You should clearly demonstrate that the project is feasible and well-motivated. Think of this proposal as if you were submitting it to a company or client requesting a data analysis and machine learning solution. You should clearly state your approach, plan, and expected outcomes. Your proposal must include:

Problem Statement and Motivation: Clearly describe the problem you intend to address and explain why this problem is important and worth solving

Dataset Description: Describe the dataset you want to use and  explain its relevance to the problem (with details on its source and contents)

Methodology: The machine learning techniques you plan to apply
Project Goals: Expected outcomes or goals of your analysis

Submission: Please submit your 2–3 page project proposal and a sample of the dataset. Be sure to review the submission instructions and the samples before uploading your files. [link]

 

2. Implement Machine Learning Techniques
Apply at least three different machine learning techniques to your dataset. Techniques such as classic regression or classification models, clustering, PCA, neural networks, CNN, LSTM, and other appropriate ML methods. Your work should demonstrate:

Data preprocessing
Feature selection or extraction
Model training
Model evaluation and improvement
 

3. Presentation
Prepare a 5–7 minute presentation to share your project with the class. The presentation should include:

Overview of the problem and dataset
Summary of the machine learning techniques applied
Key results and conclusions
Potential future work
Schedule: Please check the presentation schedule here

 

4. Final Report
Submit a 6–8 page written report that includes:

Introduction to the problem and dataset
Detailed explanation of methods used (preprocessing, models, evaluation)
Analysis of results and performance metrics
Discussion of strengths, limitations, and potential improvements
Conclusion summarizing key insights

Final Note
This project is designed to help you explore and investigate real-world machine learning problems, where problem formulation, data understanding, and methodological choices are just as important as model performance. Clear reasoning and proper methodology are essential. You are encouraged to use a real-world dataset that has been used in an existing research paper or industrial study. I do not expect excellent results or very high model performance. I do not expect you to go far beyond the course content or use state-of-the-art machine learning models. Instead, the goal is to understand how similar problems have been approached, what methods were used, and what practical challenges arise in real-world applications.
Some of you may know that when this course was previously delivered in the Winter 1 semester, students were required to collect their own data. However, since this course is now offered in the second semester of the academic year, and many of you are busy with your capstone projects, data collection is no longer required. This change is intended to make the project less time-consuming. That said, teams that have already collected their own data or are interested in doing so are very welcome to propose such projects. Depending on the scope and complexity of the project, these teams may be allowed to have additional members.
Finally, when defining your proposal and preparing your presentation and final report, do not be ambitious. Take a balanced and realistic approach—this is a course project, and I do not want you to feel unnecessary pressure. Focus on learning, applying concepts correctly, and clearly explaining your work.
